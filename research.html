Research

Research

Focus

Bayesian foundations for statistical data science

Our lab develops Bayesian methods for settings where data are scarce, noisy, high-dimensional, spatially structured, or collected under changing experimental conditions.

Our goal is to build models that provide accurate inference and prediction while communicating uncertainty and supporting reproducible data analysis.

Bayesian parametric and nonparametric models

Flexible regression, density estimation, covariance modeling, species sampling models, and structured priors.

Bayesian computational methods

Monte Carlo methods, variational approximation, Gaussian process computation, and scalable posterior inference.

Scientific, biomedical, and sensor data modeling

Applications in sensor data, biomedical studies, environmental exposure, aquaculture, and radiation-response modeling.

Spatial and structured data analysis

Spatial dependence, covariance estimation, structured shrinkage priors, and uncertainty-aware prediction.
